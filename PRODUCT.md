# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Single owner-operator (personal tool). Thai crypto trader who built this for themselves. Uses it daily to monitor live trades, review AI signals, check portfolio performance, and tune the ML models. Runs 24/7 on a server; checks in from browser on any device. Not designing for onboarding strangers — designing for deep familiarity and speed.

## Product Purpose

Aiterra is a personal AI trading system. It runs automated crypto trades across Binance/OKX/Bitkub (and Thai SET market), trains ML models in real-time, and surfaces the reasoning behind every signal. Success means the owner can glance at the dashboard and instantly know: what the AI is doing, why, how it's performing, and whether to intervene.

## Brand Personality

AI-native, precise, alive. Three words: **sentient · relentless · sharp**.

The dashboard should feel like a living intelligence — not a passive report viewer. Data pulses. Signals feel urgent. The AI feels active, not just a label. Futuristic without being decorative.

## Anti-references

- **SaaS metric dashboard** (Datadog, Grafana defaults, Notion analytics): bland card grids, white/cream backgrounds, zero trading personality — the opposite of what this should feel like.
- Any design that could belong to a generic B2B SaaS. If you could swap out the logo and call it an HR tool, it failed.

## Design Principles

1. **Data is the design** — every pixel earns its place by carrying information. Decoration competes with signal clarity and loses.
2. **Alive, not static** — the AI is running right now. The UI should reflect that: live prices, pulsing states, activity that makes the owner feel the system is breathing.
3. **Expert interface for one expert** — no hand-holding, no empty states padded with encouragement. Dense, fast, honest.
4. **Signal over noise** — BUY/SELL/HOLD is the product's core output. Typography, color, and hierarchy must make that information land in 200ms.
5. **Coherent darkness** — pure black is the canvas. Everything else is signal: neon accent for primary action, green for positive, orange for negative, mono for raw data.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Personal tool; no formal WCAG requirement. Minimum bar: body text at sufficient contrast on dark backgrounds (≥4.5:1). Reduced motion support for animations. Thai and English mixed-language content — font stack must handle both scripts cleanly.
